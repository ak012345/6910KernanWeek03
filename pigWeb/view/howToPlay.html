How to play PIG:

Items needed to play:

  • 1 six-sided die (oft. called a D6)
  • Paper and pen for keeping the score/tally
  • 2 or more players

Instructions:

  • Roll to see who goes first (highest usually goes first)
  • The First player rolls the die, If the player rolls a 2,3,4,5, or 6, they write down the number on their piece of paper.
  • as long as the player does not roll a 1, the player may continue rolling to increase their score and continue adding points. The player may pass the die to the next player at any time to lock in their score.
  • If the player rolls a 1 at any time, their turn is automatically over and they receive no points for that turn.
  • The die continues being passed around until a player reaches or surpasses the point goal (usually 100 points)

Alternative PIG game variants:

Two Dice Pig

When a player rolls two dice and one of the dies is a 1, he loses the points just for that turn. If he rolls double 1s, he loses his total score!

Big Pig

When a player rolls two dice and one of the dies is a 1, he loses the points just for that turn. If he rolls double 1s, he adds 25 to his score, but must roll again!

Big Pig Variation

When a player rolls two dice and one of the dies is a 1, he loses the points just for that turn. If he rolls double 1s, he adds 25 to his score. If he rolls any other doubles, he adds a double score to his total. For example, double 3s would score 12 (because you are doubling 3+3). After rolling a double, the player must roll again.

Sixes Pig

May be played with 1 or 2 dice, as above, but with six as the turn-ending number, instead of 1. Of course, players can choose to substitute any number for 1, as they wish.

Hog

Played as above, but with any number of dice!
